Ikhtisar Minggu 14 (Capstone)

Referensi: Henry Ch.3 (External Environment: Hypercompetition) · Henry Ch.11 (Strategic Leadership & Change) · Art.28 Chen, Lin & Michel (2010) · Art.29 Jansen, Vera & Crossan (2009)

Minggu 14 adalah capstone dari mata kuliah Manajemen Strategik Kontemporer. Dua topik yang dipilih untuk minggu final dengan sengaja:

In hypercompetitive environments, the rules of strategy change: sustainable competitive advantage becomes elusive, and the capability to continuously create and destroy advantages—led by integrated top management teams—becomes the source of superior performance. Adaptasi dari Chen, Lin & Michel — Organization Science (2010)

Tema 1 — Hypercompetition

Richard D'Aveni (1994) dalam Hypercompetition menantang premise tradisional strategi: sustainable competitive advantage. Di industri modern yang paling dinamis, advantages adalah temporary—competitor respond cepat, technology bergeser, consumer preferences berubah. Perusahaan yang berhasil di hypercompetition bukan yang build unassailable fortresses tetapi yang continuously create sequences of temporary advantages.

Chen, Lin & Michel (2010) memperdalam dengan studi empiris: action aggressiveness (frequency + intensity competitive moves) meningkatkan kinerja di hypercompetitive environments, tetapi hanya jika didukung TMT integration yang strong. Tanpa TMT integration, aggressive actions become uncoordinated dan destructive.

Tema 2 — Strategic Leadership

Upper echelons theory (Hambrick & Mason 1984): organizational outcomes reflect TMT characteristics. Strategic leadership bukan hanya soft skill—ia fundamental driver of organizational behavior dan performance.

Jansen, Vera & Crossan (2009) mengintegrasikan strategic leadership dengan tension exploration-exploitation (March 1991):

  • Transformational leadership (vision, intellectual stimulation, inspirational motivation) mendukung exploration—innovation, experimentation, new capabilities.
  • Transactional leadership (clear goals, contingent rewards, management-by-exception) mendukung exploitation—efficiency, execution, refinement of existing capabilities.

Ambidextrous leadership—kemampuan untuk enable keduanya—adalah capability puncak yang sangat sulit achieve tetapi critical di dynamic environments.

Hypercompetition Framework (D'Aveni 1994)

Richard D'Aveni dalam Hypercompetition: Managing the Dynamics of Strategic Maneuvering (1994) menawarkan perspektif yang menantang literatur strategic management dominan.

Premise: Sustainable Advantage is an Illusion

Tradisi Porter dan Barney mengasumsikan perusahaan dapat membangun sustainable competitive advantage melalui industry positioning atau VRIN resources. D'Aveni menantang:

The only advantage that is truly sustainable is the ability to create a series of temporary advantages faster than competitors can imitate them. Richard D'Aveni — Hypercompetition (1994)

Empat Arena Competitive Dynamics

D'Aveni identifikasi empat arena di mana competitive interactions terjadi:

1. Cost & Quality Arena

Kompetisi tradisional—Porter's generic strategies. Incumbents dapat membangun scale advantages, quality reputations. Namun di hypercompetition: competitor dengan quick imitation dapat erode advantages rapidly.

2. Timing & Know-How Arena

First-mover advantages, knowledge leadership. Di hypercompetition: first-movers face risk of "first-mover disadvantage"—educating market yang kemudian dimasuki fast followers yang leverage insights.

3. Strongholds Arena

Building defensive positions via economies of scale, deep pockets, geographic dominance. Di hypercompetition: deep pockets dapat drain jika prolonged war; geographic strongholds tersinggung oleh digital disruption.

4. Deep Pockets Arena

Financial staying power. Di hypercompetition: capital markets reward growth + paths to profitability—deep pockets tanpa strategic direction tidak sufficient.

Implikasi untuk Strategic Management

1. Shift dari "Defend" ke "Attack"

Traditional: build competitive advantage, defend it. Hypercompetition: continuously create new advantages, even if existing ones still viable. "Continuous disruption of own advantages" sebelum competitor.

2. Accept Temporary Advantages as Norm

Instead of searching for "unassailable" positions, accept that advantages are temporary. Build capability untuk create-exploit-abandon cycles faster than competitors.

3. Aggressive Action as Default

In stable environments, passive/reactive strategies viable. In hypercompetition, passive = slow death. Aggressive action—price moves, product launches, strategic alliances, M&A—becomes the norm.

Industri yang Paling Hypercompetitive

  • Tech platforms: advantages eroded quickly by new entrants or established players pivoting.
  • Fintech: regulasi evolving, new entrants constant, customer loyalty low.
  • E-commerce: platform consolidation + shakeout + live commerce disruption.
  • Media digital: advertising revenue volatile, consumer attention fragmented.
  • Smartphone manufacturing: rapid technology cycles, commoditization risk.

Industri yang Relatif Bukan Hypercompetitive

  • Utilities (PLN): regulated monopoly/oligopoly.
  • Heavy infrastructure: capital intensity + long cycles.
  • Consumer staples (Indofood): moderate change, strong brand moats.
  • Perbankan konsumer Indonesia (4 bank besar): regulated, moderate digital disruption (tapi meningkat).

Chen, Lin & Michel (2010) — Empirical Validation

Penelitian empiris mereka menunjukkan:

Finding 1 — Action Aggressiveness Matters di Hypercompetition

Firms dengan higher action aggressiveness (more frequent + intense competitive moves) achieve better performance in hypercompetitive environments. Passive firms mengalami decline.

Finding 2 — TMT Integration as Moderator

Aggressive actions without TMT integration = chaos. Actions uncoordinated, conflicting signals, execution failures. TMT integration = prerequisite for harnessing aggressive action into coherent strategic advantage.

Finding 3 — Context Specificity

Action aggressiveness beneficial terutama di hypercompetitive; di stable environments, over-aggressive actions dapat menjadi costly waste.

Kritik terhadap Hypercompetition Framework

  • Overstatement: tidak semua industri adalah hypercompetitive; banyak traditional industries yang tetap stable.
  • Selection bias: cases yang dianalisis D'Aveni adalah industri tech + consumer electronics—tidak generalize untuk semua.
  • Burnout risk: continuous aggressive action dapat exhaust organization—sustainability questioned.
  • Self-fulfilling prophecy: jika semua perusahaan mengadopsi hypercompetition mindset, industri menjadi lebih destructive untuk semua.

Keseimbangan Pragmatis

Most realistic pandangan: spectrum of competitive intensity. Some industries (perbankan konsumer, semen, consumer staples) mendekati stable; some (tech platforms, fintech) mendekati hypercompetitive. Strategic management yang canggih: diagnose where industry is, calibrate strategy accordingly.

Upper Echelons Theory (Hambrick & Mason 1984)

Donald Hambrick & Phyllis Mason dalam artikel seminal Academy of Management Review (1984) mengusulkan upper echelons theory: organizations are a reflection of their top managers.

Inti Argumen

Strategic decisions di organisasi kompleks sering complicated—involving ambiguity, uncertainty, multiple interpretations. Dalam kondisi ini, cognitive base dari decision-makers (top management team) menjadi critical. Manager membawa:

Demographic Characteristics

  • Age: older managers cenderung risk-averse.
  • Tenure: longer tenure cenderung commit ke status quo.
  • Functional background: finance-heavy TMT cenderung defensive, marketing-heavy cenderung expansive.
  • Education: formal education levels correlate dengan strategic choices.
  • Socioeconomic background: affect risk tolerance, stakeholder orientation.

Psychological Characteristics

  • Cognitive base: mental models dari past experiences.
  • Values: what managers consider important (profit, growth, social impact).
  • Risk tolerance: willingness to take bold actions.

Implikasi

Organizational outcomes—strategy choices, innovation rate, financial performance, response to crisis—reflect TMT characteristics. Untuk understand organization, study its TMT.

Research Evidence

Decades of research menguji upper echelons:

  • TMT functional diversity → strategic diversity dan innovation.
  • TMT tenure homogeneity → strategic persistence (resistance to change).
  • TMT age heterogeneity → better adaptation to dynamic environments.
  • TMT education → strategic sophistication + scanning behavior.

TMT Integration (Chen, Lin & Michel 2010)

Selain characteristics, TMT integration—bagaimana TMT berfungsi sebagai unit—matters significantly.

Dimensions of TMT Integration

  • Shared vision: alignment pada strategic direction.
  • Communication quality: open dialogue, information sharing.
  • Decision-making coordination: integrated decisions, not siloed.
  • Collaborative behavior: mutual support, constructive conflict.
  • Role clarity: understanding each other's responsibilities.

Why TMT Integration Matters in Hypercompetition

Di hypercompetitive environments yang memerlukan aggressive action:

  • Speed: integrated TMT dapat decide + execute cepat.
  • Coherence: aggressive actions consistent, tidak contradict.
  • Resilience: ketika actions fail (inevitable in hypercompetition), integrated TMT recover together.
  • Learning: integrated TMT extract lessons dari outcomes, improve future decisions.
TMT yang fragmented menghasilkan:
  • Slow decisions (political conflicts).
  • Contradictory signals to organization.
  • Execution failures (different TMT members pulling different directions).
  • Blame culture when actions fail.

Aplikasi ke Indonesia

BCA — High TMT Integration

Jahja Setiaatmadja sebagai Presdir sejak 2011—kontinuitas kepemimpinan yang langka. TMT BCA consistently integrated via shared SMART values, internal promotion culture (executives grew together), predictable decision processes. Hasil: execution yang konsisten dan strong performance selama 2 decades.

Garuda Indonesia — Low TMT Integration (Historis)

Pre-2020 Garuda: multiple CEO changes, politik yang kompleks (BUMN dengan pressure politis), conflicting priorities. TMT fragmented antara commercial vs. government mandate perspectives. Contributing factor untuk crisis yang accumulated.

GoTo — TMT Integration Challenge Post-Merger

Merger Gojek + Tokopedia (2021) menciptakan TMT integration challenge—different corporate cultures (Gojek as technical ops, Tokopedia as marketplace), different decision processes, different leadership styles. Post-merger integration journey masih berjalan. Kinerja post-pivot yang solid menunjukkan TMT integration sedang membaik.

Implikasi untuk Strategic Management

1. Hire, Develop, Manage TMT Carefully

TMT composition = strategic decision. Siapa yang ada di TMT (background, values, styles) akan shape strategic behavior organization for years to come.

2. Build TMT Integration Deliberately

Integration tidak spontan. Invest dalam: TMT retreats, communication norms, shared planning processes, conflict resolution mechanisms.

3. Diversity Plus Integration

TMT yang homogeneous dan integrated dapat groupthink. TMT yang diverse tetapi fragmented chaos. Sweet spot: diverse perspectives plus integrated functioning. Hardest untuk achieve—membutuhkan intentional leadership.

4. Succession Planning as Strategic Priority

TMT transitions adalah moments of vulnerability. Good strategic management include rigorous succession planning untuk maintain integration across leadership changes.

Exploration-Exploitation Tension (March 1991)

James March dalam artikel klasik Organization Science (1991) mengidentifikasi tension fundamental dalam organizational learning:

Exploration

Search, variation, experimentation, discovery. Activities yang explore new possibilities, develop new capabilities, create future options. Karakteristik: uncertain returns, long time horizons, high variance outcomes. Example: R&D, new market entry, disruptive technology development.

Exploitation

Refinement, efficiency, execution, selection of known alternatives. Activities yang exploit existing capabilities for current returns. Karakteristik: certain returns, short time horizons, low variance outcomes. Example: operational improvements, cost reduction, scaling proven products.

Tension

Organizations allocate finite resources (capital, talent, management attention). Resources untuk exploration berkurang exploitation. Resources untuk exploitation berkurang exploration. Tradeoff.

The basic problem confronting an organization is to engage in sufficient exploitation to ensure its current viability and, at the same time, devote enough energy to exploration to ensure its future viability. James March — Organization Science (1991)

Consequences of Imbalance

Too Much Exploration

Over-experimentation, too many unfinished initiatives, financial strain dari R&D tanpa returns. Company tidak extract value dari existing capabilities. Contoh: startup yang continuously pivot tanpa ever monetize product.

Too Much Exploitation

Efficient but ossified. Company menjadi rigid, tidak adapt to environment changes. Classic "innovator's dilemma" (Christensen 1997). Contoh: Nokia/BlackBerry yang terlalu fokus pada existing handset business sementara smartphone revolution terjadi.

Organizational Ambidexterity

Ambidexterity: capability untuk do both exploration + exploitation simultaneously.

Structural Ambidexterity

Separate organizational units untuk exploration vs. exploitation. Exploration unit (R&D, innovation labs) dengan different culture, timeline, success metrics. Exploitation unit (core business) dengan efficiency focus. Contoh: Astra memiliki core mature businesses + exploratory investments (Astra Tol Nusantara, EV investments).

Contextual Ambidexterity

Same units do both—individuals trained to switch between exploration dan exploitation depending on context. Requires cultural sophistication. Contoh: Apple employees bekerja pada existing iPhone improvements sambil develop next-gen products.

Temporal Ambidexterity

Organization cycles between exploration dan exploitation phases. Periods of consolidation (exploitation) alternate with periods of innovation (exploration). Less common but sometimes strategic choice.

Jansen, Vera & Crossan (2009) — Leadership for Ambidexterity

Jansen, Vera & Crossan (Leadership Quarterly 2009) mengintegrasikan exploration-exploitation dengan leadership theory.

Framework

Gambar 14.1 — Jansen, Vera & Crossan Ambidextrous Leadership Framework

Transformational Leadership → Exploration

Why Transformational Drives Exploration

Vision & Inspiration: articulate future possibilities yang menarik followers untuk invest dalam uncertain new directions.

Intellectual Stimulation: challenge assumptions, encourage creative thinking, welcome diverse perspectives.

Idealized Influence: role-model risk-taking dan learning orientation.

Individualized Consideration: support individual development, tolerate failure sebagai learning.

Transformational leaders inspire organization untuk undertake exploration—even when short-term returns unclear.

Transactional Leadership → Exploitation

Why Transactional Drives Exploitation

Clear Goals: specific targets untuk execution excellence.

Contingent Rewards: link performance to rewards—motivates focus pada measurable outcomes.

Management-by-Exception: intervene when standards not met—ensures execution discipline.

Performance Monitoring: track metrics, enforce accountability.

Transactional leaders operationalize strategy—ensure execution pada existing capabilities dengan efficiency.

Environmental Dynamism sebagai Moderator

Jansen et al. finding: leadership style impact dimoderasi oleh environmental dynamism.

High Environmental Dynamism (Hypercompetition)

Exploration is critical—adaptive capabilities diperlukan untuk survive. Transformational leadership memiliki stronger positive impact. Transactional leadership alone insufficient—organization akan tertinggal.

Low Environmental Dynamism (Stable)

Exploitation sufficient—efficiency matters more. Transactional leadership dengan stronger returns. Transformational leadership yang over-emphasize exploration dapat menjadi distraction.

Ambidextrous Leadership Implementation

Single Leader dengan Both Styles

Rare tetapi possible—leaders yang dapat switch style based on context. Contoh: Satya Nadella di Microsoft (transformational untuk cloud pivot, transactional untuk execution).

Complementary TMT Members

Different TMT members dengan different styles—CEO transformational, COO transactional. Requires TMT integration (Chen-Lin-Michel 2010) untuk synergy, bukan conflict.

Level-Specific Leadership

Transformational at higher org levels (strategy), transactional at middle/operational levels (execution). Cascading leadership architecture.

Aplikasi ke Indonesia

Jahja Setiaatmadja di BCA — Balanced Approach

Long-tenure CEO yang shows elements of both. Transformational: digital transformation vision, SMART culture building. Transactional: clear KPIs, consistent performance management, branch network execution discipline. Berada di middle-level environmental dynamism (perbankan moderate)—balanced approach fits.

Andre Soelistyo at GoTo — Transformational (Era Post-Merger)

Facing hypercompetitive environment pasca-merger, needed transformational leadership untuk align Gojek + Tokopedia cultures dan explore new directions (fintech integration, TikTok partnership). Still developing TMT integration.

Ririek Adriansyah di Telkom Indonesia — Transactional

BUMN telco yang operations-heavy. Focus pada operational excellence, execution, performance management. Appropriate untuk relatively stable legacy business sambil exploring 5G + digital adjacencies. TMT complementary members handle exploration.

Pemeriksa Konsep — Minggu 14 (Capstone)

Pemeriksa Konsep · Hypercompetition & Strategic Leadership

K1. D'Aveni's hypercompetition: apakah ini descriptif (semakin banyak industri menjadi hypercompetitive) atau prescriptif (managers harus bertindak as if in hypercompetition)? Konsekuensi untuk Indonesia yang ada industri stable dan industri hypercompetitive?

K2. Chen, Lin & Michel: action aggressiveness × TMT integration. Pilih satu kasus Indonesia di mana aggressive action tanpa TMT integration memberikan hasil negative. Pertahankan.

K3. Jansen et al. ambidextrous leadership. Apakah mungkin one person genuinely ambidextrous? Atau ambidexterity harus di level TMT? Debat dengan contoh.

K4. March exploration-exploitation: apakah ini truly zero-sum tradeoff, atau dapat di-transcend via organizational design? Pertahankan dengan contoh perusahaan Indonesia.

K5. Capstone Integration Question: Pilih satu perusahaan Indonesia. Lakukan full strategic analysis menggunakan minimum 8 kerangka dari 14 minggu mata kuliah. Sintesiskan insights ke single strategic recommendation. Pertahankan dengan rigor akademis.
