## Enam Konsep Inti — Minggu 14

<div class="concept-card">
<h4>1. Hypercompetition (D'Aveni 1994)</h4>
<p>Richard D'Aveni dalam <em>Hypercompetition</em> (1994) mendefinisikan hypercompetition sebagai environment di mana:
<ul>
<li>Competitive advantages <em>temporary</em>—terkikis cepat.</li>
<li>Competitive interactions <em>aggressive, frequent, unpredictable</em>.</li>
<li>Traditional sources of sustainable advantage (economies of scale, brand, regulasi) <em>insufficient</em>.</li>
</ul>
Industri yang paling hypercompetitive: tech platforms, media digital, fintech, e-commerce. Key insight: dalam hypercompetition, strategi shift dari "defend existing advantage" ke "continuously create new temporary advantages".</p>
</div>

<div class="concept-card">
<h4>2. Action Aggressiveness</h4>
<p>Frekuensi dan intensitas competitive actions (price changes, product launches, advertising campaigns, strategic alliances). Chen, Lin &amp; Michel (2010) menunjukkan: di hypercompetitive environments, firms dengan higher action aggressiveness mencapai better performance—asalkan didukung TMT integration yang baik. Without TMT integration, aggressive actions become uncoordinated dan destructive.</p>
</div>

<div class="concept-card">
<h4>3. Upper Echelons Theory (Hambrick &amp; Mason 1984)</h4>
<p>Organizational outcomes—strategic choices, performance—adalah reflection dari characteristics top management team (TMT): age, tenure, functional background, education, cognitive base. TMT sebagai <em>strategic actor</em>, bukan hanya "the executives". Implikasi: studying leadership characteristics membantu predicting organizational behavior.</p>
</div>

<div class="concept-card">
<h4>4. TMT Integration (Chen, Lin &amp; Michel 2010)</h4>
<p>Seberapa baik top management team berfungsi sebagai <em>integrated unit</em>—shared vision, communication quality, decision-making coordination, collaborative behavior. TMT integration memoderasi relationship antara action aggressiveness dan performance. Di hypercompetitive environments, TMT yang integrated dapat eksekusi aggressive actions effectively; TMT yang fragmented justru menghasilkan chaos.</p>
</div>

<div class="concept-card">
<h4>5. Exploration vs. Exploitation (March 1991)</h4>
<p>James March (1991) mengidentifikasi dua aktivitas fundamentally different:
<ul>
<li><strong>Exploration:</strong> search, variation, experimentation, discovery of new opportunities. Future-oriented, uncertain returns.</li>
<li><strong>Exploitation:</strong> refinement, efficiency, execution, optimization of current opportunities. Present-oriented, certain returns.</li>
</ul>
Tension: resources allocated to one reduce resources for the other. <em>Organizational ambidexterity</em>—kemampuan melakukan keduanya simultaneously—adalah capability yang sangat sulit.</p>
</div>

<div class="concept-card">
<h4>6. Ambidextrous Leadership (Jansen, Vera &amp; Crossan 2009)</h4>
<p>Transformational leadership (vision, inspiration, intellectual stimulation) mendukung <em>exploration</em>. Transactional leadership (clear goals, contingent rewards, management-by-exception) mendukung <em>exploitation</em>. Ambidextrous leaders dapat switch antara keduanya—atau different leaders dalam TMT exhibit different styles. Dimoderasi oleh environmental dynamism: di dynamic environments, exploration lebih valued; di stable environments, exploitation lebih cukup.</p>
</div>
